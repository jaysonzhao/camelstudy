Agile Integration in Action

SaaS Integrations across Hybrid Environments 

  • 74% of enterprises rely on at least one SaaS solution

  • SaaS applications are now key business assets

  • Must be integrated with core systems and amongst themselves

  • Often purchased and operated by different departments

  • Highly heterogeneous data models and workflows

Challenges: multiple departments and products, security, enforcement of IT rules, long term maintenance

Agile Integration in Action

Partner Integration

  • Business to Business partnership amplifies reach

  • Create new channels and revenue sources

  • Integrate with partners quicker, cheaper and more effectively

  • Leverage winner-takes-all dynamics and increase stickiness with partners and market share

  • Avoid custom integrations per partner but instead provide standard interfaces

  • Monetize partner relationships

Challenges: creating different partner tiers with different scopes of access, access control, interface documentation, avoiding customer integrations per partner, demand-based scaling, platform evolution/change management, creating secure monetization models

Agile Integration in Action

Customer Integration

  • More customers expecting frictionless access to data

  • It has become critical to enable customers to integrate directly with your business

  • Allowing direct access to data and transactions means retention of customers is higher and transaction volumes increase

  • Revenue opportunity for the company to have bulk access for the company

  • Customer API programs which support this business approach need to be well managed and effective

Challenges: internal systems are often not designed for reuse, let alone external use, unexpected requirements, customer success becomes an important factor to consider

Agile Integration in Action

Enabling Customer Facing Apps

  • Customer application strategies now draw heavily on internal IT

  • Mobile and IoT both drive external access to systems, as do omni-channel strategies

  • This means integrations need to deliver the right data to them and scale

  • Previously responsibility of engineering, but now it often depends on IT

  • Generally also links in user databases, billing, and other internal functions

Challenges: user management, security, scaling is a challenge with more volume, pressure for quick iteration of backend to match application development needs, pressure to handle scale
